# You are not your inner critic

#status/done

Related to [[Love your authentic self by believing you are enough]]

Authenticity and self-acceptance is learning to recognize what is your voice amongst the messages of the many, telling you how you should behave or think. We choose to discard what is not relevant to us. But this doesn't come easy nor without [[Active practice leads to skill development|active practice]] through practices such as [[Meditation is the practice of mindfulness|meditation]].

Most of us will have an [[Let negative thoughts flow by with free attention|inner voice critiquing]] us which is the gestalt of all the [[Shame and guilt are inherited|negative messages we have incorporated subconsciously into our minds]] thus far. Often times, it may require us to sit over our [[Staying with your emotional center of gravity|emotional center of gravity]] or engage in the [[Use the disputation strategy to overcome pessimism through facts|disputation strategy to overcome pessimism through facts]].

Just as water will follow the course of the current, going uphill and fighting our inner nature is not simple. It needs to become a [[New habits are a result of deliberate practice|become a habit]].

See also [[Automatic thoughts are suggestions not orders|Automatic thoughts are mere suggestions of the mind rather than orders]]

## Our inner critic stems from past external voices

For most people, we will possess a [[Inner monologue is the process of silently speaking meaningful thoughts|constant stream of thoughts]], some of it positive while at times it may be negative and even harsh/cruel.

Inner voices were once outer voices, from authority figures such as parents, siblings, bullies or teachers which became ingrained in our ways of thinking. Therein lies their psychological origins of the inner voice which have become [[Cognitive distortions|cognitive distortions]].

Often times, it stems from a combination of [[Cultural roots of shame|cultural messages]] as well as the [[Personal experiences are unique like a garden|unique circumstances of our upbringing]] which become internalized as [[Early emotional wounds are negative early life experiences|early emotional wounds]].

[[Parents have a profound impact on child development|Our attachment figures' behaviours]], [[Freeze Fight or Flight Response to danger|how they cope and respond to stress]] becomes internalized within the child's memory imprint. [[Unloving parents generate self-hatred in their children|Being unloved as a child may lead one to constantly question themselves and their own flaws]]. It can also lead to a lot of [[Disliking yourself through self-hatred and self-blame|self-hatred]].

You can [[Healing from emotional pain is a choice|choose to heal from this emotional pain]] once you've gotten to its roots.. We can learn to manage our inner critic by engaging in therapy, use [[Cognitive restructuring is the process of adopting healthier perspectives|cognitive rescripting techniques]] or [[Staying with your emotional center of gravity|staying over your emotional center of gravity]].
